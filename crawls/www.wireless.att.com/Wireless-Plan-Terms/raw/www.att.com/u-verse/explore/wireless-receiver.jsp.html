AT&T U-verse® TV Wireless Receiver

Watch TV virtually anywhere in your home with the NEW Wireless Receiver - Exclusively from AT&T.



  • Arrange your living room the way you want it.
  • Place your TV in the kitchen, even the garage – no TV outlet needed.

  • Move another TV into the family room so everyone has the best seat.
  • Watch your favorite team on the patio while grilling out with friends.

  • AT&T U-verse gives you the freedom to place your receiver where you want, regardless of where your existing TV outlets are located.
  • Wireless signal strength indicator to aid in placement.
  • Streams live HD & SD TV content.
  • Functions like a standard receiver including, Total Home DVR®, U-verse Movies, and Pay-Per-View.
  • Access to Applications, Multiviews, Channel Guide, Menu, and your other favorite features.
  • Installation is fast and easy.

See if you qualify for U-verse and view the latest special offers.
During your installation, ask your technician about upgrading to the Wireless Receiver.

Existing U-verse Customers
If you already have U-verse TV installed in your home and want to add a wireless receiver, log in to your account and follow the on-screen instructions.
  • Wireless Receiver
  • Wireless Access Point
  • Power outlet and connection to TV required.
  • Maximum of two wireless TV receivers per household.
  • Requires Residential Gateway**
  • Wireless video performance subject to interference and environmental factors.

* Wireless Receiver: A one-time fee of $49, and monthly recurring fee of $7, per wireless receiver applies. Wireless from the Wireless Access Point to the Wireless Receiver; power outlet and separate connection of Wireless Receiver to TV is required. For residential use in a single household only. Limit 2 wireless receivers per household. Customer responsible for use of Wireless Receiver. Actual range of wireless signal will vary and may be limited by several factors including, but not limited to electromagnetic interference, home construction material, obstructions, and other environmental factors.

** Some customers may have an Intelligent Network Interface Device (iNID) instead of a Residential Gateway. The wireless receiver is not compatible with an iNID.
